Mohammad I. Kawas MD, PhD is an Associate Research Scientist. He completed his PhD in Neuroscience at RIIPL, where he continues his postdoctoral training. During his doctoral work, he contributed to a wide range of projects focusing on BOLD imaging and functional connectivity across human cohorts spanning from neonates to older adults. He also participated in studies involving non-human primates in social housing environments, examining the effects of hierarchical stress on insular structure and function.

His primary PhD research focused on investigating the impact of repetitive head impacts sustained during a high school football season on brain network connectivity and markers of neuroplasticity.

During his postdoctoral training, Mohammad was awarded the StrokeNet fellowship, which he held for two years. In this role, he analyzed clinically acquired MRI data to quantify brain volumetrics associated with predicting post-stroke outcomes and recovery. He also conducted bedside assessments of pulse wave velocity to evaluate its utility in predicting outcomes in patients with hemorrhagic stroke.

More recently, Mohammad has been examining the utility of the Default Mode Network Failure Quotient (NFQ) as a predictor of cognitive status in an elderly cohort from the Wake Forest Alzheimer's Disease Research Center (ADRC), supported by pilot grant funding from the center. He is currently leveraging his clinical expertise and neuroimaging experience to develop clinical and research applications for AI-assisted analysis of MRI and CT imaging data.
